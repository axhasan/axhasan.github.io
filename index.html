Musings
March 8, 2026
We are currently navigating the ‘Great Decoupling’ of AI: where astronomical capex finally meets the friction of the real-world labor market. While the value proposition of AI is undeniable—unlocking non-linear gains in drug discovery, energy management, and software architecture—the human cost is no longer theoretical. With over 150,000 job cuts in the first two months of 2026 alone, the risk isn’t just ‘replacement,’ but the erosion of entry-level mentorship and wage stagnation in automated sectors. We should expect a ‘barbell’ job market: a surge in high-touch human roles and elite AI-orchestrators, with a hollowed-out middle. The next 18 months will determine if AI is a tide that lifts all boats or a flood that only the most capital-heavy can weather.
